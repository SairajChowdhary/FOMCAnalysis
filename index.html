Anatomy of the FOMC Release

The chart data confirms a classic, three-stage pattern of market behavior during this high-impact event.

1. Pre-Release Tension

In the 10 minutes prior to 2:00 PM ET, volume was suppressed and price action was muted, indicating market-wide anticipation and risk reduction.

2. The Volatility Event

At the exact moment of release, volume surged over 15x the prior minute's average, and price experienced its most violent move of the day.

3. Post-Release Digestion

Following the initial spike, volume remained highly elevated as institutions repositioned, and price began a new, more orderly trend-finding phase.

Applicable Risk Framework

The observed data validates the need for a strict, event-driven risk management protocol.

Position Reduction

The extreme price spike confirms that holding discretionary size into the event is untenable. A flat or significantly reduced position is mandatory.

Algorithmic Halts

The 800% spread increase would render most algorithmic entry signals unprofitable due to slippage. Halting automated systems is critical.

Stop-Loss Invalidation

The 35.75 point move in under two minutes would have triggered any standard stop-loss. This highlights their unreliability during such events.

Limit Order Execution

With spreads widening dramatically, market orders would have resulted in catastrophic fills. Only limit orders provide necessary execution control.
